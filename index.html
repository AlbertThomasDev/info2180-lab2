Why do Electric Cars Accelerate Faster

October 2, 2024 by Albert Thomas

To answer this question we have to know how both types of vehicles move. Like gas cars, electric vehicles need energy to move, and they get their energy from their appropriate sources. EVs get their energy from batteries in the form of electrical energy and Gas cars get their energy from gasoline in the form of chemical energy. Vehicles need a mechanism to convert their stored energy into mechanical energy and they achieve this by using electric motors for EVs, and Internal Combustion Engines for gas cars. EVs use their electric motors to convert electrical energy into mechanical energy, and gasoline Cars use internal combustion engines (ICE) to convert chemical energy into mechanical energy.

Now, the main reason why electric cars accelerate faster is because of torque, the force needed to rotate an object about an axis. Electric Motors deliver maximum torque instantly from the start, thanks to their efficient design. They can convert a higher percentage of stored energy into motion, making them more efficient than ICEs. Gasoline Engines, on the other hand, are less efficient. They need to reach high RPMs to deliver peak torque, resulting in slower acceleration. But Why are they less efficient?

Internal Combustion Engines (ICEs) convert only about 20-30% of chemical energy into mechanical energy. The rest is lost. It’s primarily lost as heat through exhaust gases, cooling systems, and friction within engine components. This isn’t the same with electric cars which convert over 90% of their stored energy into motion. These losses reduce the overall efficiency of the engine, affecting both power delivery and torque generation.

So, to summarise, Electric Vehicles are faster because they are more efficient when converting energy, and have the advantage of instant torque. Gasoline powered vehicles just are not efficient enough when converting energy which affects their performance.

Is your Phone actually Waterproof?

October 3, 2024 by Albert Thomas

Your phone is not waterproof. Now obviously you’d probably think I'm lying, especially because of what you’ve seen on the internet. But there are a lot of misconceptions about water, and phones. And it all lies in the marketing. The words waterproof and water- resistant do not mean the same thing. Infact, the word “waterproof” isn’t even used in the marketing of your phones, water resistant is. Consumers see this word and automatically assume that it has the same meaning as the word waterproof, but this isn’t the case. Waterproof means that water just simply can’t get in it. Water resistant means that it can resist the penetration of water to a degree, but not entirely.

Now, they actually rate water resistance by something called the IP Code or Ingress Protection Code, which indicates how well a device is protected against dust and water. The IP rating is made up of two digits. The first digit represents protection against dust, and the second digit represents protection against liquids. Phones like the iphone 15 pro and the Samsung Galaxy s23 Ultra have an IP68 rating. This means the phone is Protected from dust and can survive in deep water between 1 to 1.5 metres for about 30 minutes. However, even with this rating, water resistance is not guaranteed.

In reality, your phone faces unpredictable environments. Factors like pressure and temperature can significantly impact how well your device handles exposure. Manufacturers do equip their devices with water-resistant features, but it’s essential to understand the limitations of these protections. Yes, you can bring your phone in water but it can still get damaged because no phone is truly waterproof.
